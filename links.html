Teaching Notebooks

The following notebooks are from a one day environmental DNA (eDNA) workshop, given as part of the Otago Carpentries Spring School. The eDNA workshop was team taught by Lara Urban, Gert-Jan Jeunen, and myself. I organised the workshop. All three contributed to content creation; the first section, QC and Denoising was taught by Gert-Jan; I covered the Taxonomy Assignment section; and Lara covered Statistical Analysis. I prepared all material for publication on the website. The material is also available on the Spring School website (Day 5). All of these lessons were taught using Jupyter Hub on the NeSI server, which will now be the model for future workshops through Otago Carpentries.

Finding Haplotypes in Alignments

The following three notebooks were used to create scripts for a project to determine frequencies of closely related haplotypes in a mock community experiment, in order to evaluate efficacy of denoising/clustering algorithms. The first two also server to illustrate my approach to script development. For larger scripts, my first step is usually to work out the different steps, using one or two example files or samples.
